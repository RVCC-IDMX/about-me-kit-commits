About Me

I grew up around the Bell Labs area in New Jersey. I’ve met a really interesting assortment of Physics PhD students, mathematicians, developers, and professional designers over the years. I also grew up around a lot of people who worked on technology projects that were funded by the U.S. military and I’ve worked on a number of projects under NDA agreements, and I have learned the “real world” version of working with clients. One of the trends I’m seeing in the intersection of freelance developer work and cybersecurity work is an agreement that sometimes you learn to be more selective about what clients you will and will not work with not to be “snobby” or “pretentious”, it’s just that with the way that the cyber landscape is right now, you just have to be careful to not take on “just any client” for financial reasons. It’s ok to gradually learn how to make work that has a more widespread and commercial appeal. There’s no shame at all in starting to join projects, companies, and teams that are well funded. There’s no shame in being a developer who also looks at stock patterns. Some developers and designers have just seen enough of the “well that part of the internet is on fire today, anyways…” side of tech that “we’re just kind of in it for dog, lawn, immediate family members, and some close friends” at this point.

This blog Article is on Stacking with Z-index

What is the z-index?

The z-index is a CSS property that controls the vertical stacking order of elements that overlap on a web page. It determines which elements appear in front of or behind others when they occupy the same space. The z-index is similar to layers in photoshop. The higher the z-index,the closer to the viewer the element appears. The lower the z-index,the further away from the viewer the content appears.


Why z-index matters as a job skill

These are the following reasons why z-index is an important job skill for web developers and designers to have.The z-index impacts dropdown menus,navigation bars, modals,pop-ups, side panels, tooltips, sticky headers, and image overlays. If a developer does not understand stacking, these features will oftentimes vanish, appear behind other content, block clicks, and break layouts.


Why do employers value z-index knowledge?

Build better user experiences: Understanding z-index. 1. The z-index allows developers to create more usable and visually appealing interfaces by ensuring that important elements are always visible and accessible. 2. Troubleshoot layout issues: Developers who understand z-index can quickly identify and resolve layout problems related to overlapping elements. 3.The Z-index allows developers to ship professional quality UI/UX design work. 4. Understanding the z-index will allow developers to avoid common front end bugs. Some common front end bugs that the z-index can help avoid include: dropdown menus that disappear behind other content, modals appearing behind the main page content, sticky headers covered by scrolling content, images overlaping text incorrectly, hover effects hidden behind elements, forms blocked by invisible layers, floating buttons hidden, toast notifications not available, video or ads overlapping UI controls, and tooltips that are hidden or misplaced.


What Do Recruiters Like To Listen For

"I used the z-index property to manage the stacking order of overlapping elements on a web page.I used z-index to fix modal stacking issues, create invisible dropdowns, fix blocked forms,and fix layering bugs caused by stacking contexts."

"Where should you put your knowledge of z-indexes on your resume?" You can include your knowledge of z-indexes in the skills section of your resume. List it under "UI Layering and Debugging","CSS Stacking Contexts Control", "Z-Index Bug Fixing",and "Layout Visibility Troubleshooting".
